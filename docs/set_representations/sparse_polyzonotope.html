Sparse Polynomial Zonotope

Definition​

PZ≔{∑i=1h(∏k=1pαkE(k,i))G(⋅,i)D+∑j=1qβjG(⋅,j)I ∣ αk,βj∈[−1,1]}{\cal PZ} \coloneqq \left\{ \sum_{i=1}^{h} ( \prod_{k=1}^{p} \alpha_{k}^{E_{(k,i)}} ) G_{(\cdot, i)}^{D} + \sum_{j=1}^{q} \beta_{j} G_{(\cdot, j)}^{I} \ \big| \ \alpha_{k}, \beta_{j} \in [-1,1] \right\}PZ:={i=1∑h​(k=1∏p​αkE(k,i)​​)G(⋅,i)D​+j=1∑q​βj​G(⋅,j)I​ ∣∣​ αk​,βj​∈[−1,1]}

shorthand as PZ=<GD,GI,E,U>PZ∈Rn{\cal PZ} = \left< G^{D}, G^{I},E,\mathrm{U}\right>_{\cal PZ} \in \R^{n}PZ=⟨GD,GI,E,U⟩PZ​∈Rn, where U∈N>01×p\mathrm{U} \in \N_{> 0}^{1 \times p}U∈N>01×p​ introduced as a row vector storing all unambiguous integer identifier for each dependent factor αk\alpha_{k}αk​.

Example​

PZ=<[42124022],[10],[01030011],[01]>PZ{\cal PZ}=\left< \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 2 & 1 & 2 \\ 4 & 0 & 2 & 2 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}, \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \right>_{\cal PZ}PZ=⟨[44​20​12​22​],[10​],[00​10​01​31​],[0​1​]⟩PZ​

defines the set

PZ={[44]+[20]α0+[12]α1+[22]α03α1+[10]β0 ∣ α0,α1,β0∈[−1,1]}{\cal PZ}=\left\{ \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \alpha_{0} + \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \alpha_{1} + \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \alpha_{0}^{3} \alpha_{1} + \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \beta_{0} \ \bigg| \ \alpha_{0},\alpha_{1}, \beta_{0} \in [-1,1] \right\}PZ={[44​]+[20​]α0​+[12​]α1​+[22​]α03​α1​+[10​]β0​ ∣∣​ α0​,α1​,β0​∈[−1,1]}
